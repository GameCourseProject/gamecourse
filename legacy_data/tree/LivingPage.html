Living Page

Dead-tree books are sooooo eighteenth century... Today, we're moving towards ebooks! And even current ebooks aren't all that great. You see, they try to mimic their dead tree counterparts with static pages full of text. We can do better.

In particular, there is this notion of interactive books or reactive documents, where we can interact with the page. The goal is to augment the reading experience to make materials easier to understand. This has huge implications for teaching, for instance. The idea is that instead of just reading an equation on paper, you can change its values and see the result right there in real time. If you manipulate a graph, you get to see the text changing to refer the values you've just changed.

Former Apple designer Bret Victor has been playing with these ideas for a while. He created Tangle, a javascript library to do this on the browser. Check it out at his site, Worry Dream, as well as some other examples in his Explorable Explanations and Kill Math pages.

Your goal here is to choose a suitable technical subject of your liking and create a reactive document to explain it. Your document must contain not only reactive text but also graphs, figures or diagrams. You can use Tangle or whatever else you like, as long as the result is similar.

Post a description of the subject you're tackling and the design process of the document. Attach a zip with a self-contained document (i.e., include all files needed to make it work). Be original and, as always, we may ask for improvements/changes before accepting the result, especially if the quality or originality requirements aren't met.
