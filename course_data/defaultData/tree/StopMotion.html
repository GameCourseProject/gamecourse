Stop Motion

Stop Motion is an animation technique that consists in taking a series of still photos, where the characters/background are physically manipulated to appear to move on their own. Changes between consecutive frames are small and incremental, and are then played back as a continuous sequence. South Park and Wallace and Gromit are two notable examples, but people have been doing this with all sorts of characters. LEGO, even:

Claymation is another way to go. Take some modelling clay and be creative with it.

Make sure your camera isn't moving all around the place (handheld won't work) unless you're going for a "Blair Witch Project" vibe, and try to get a constant and controlled lighting

So write or choose a story, find some dolls, action figures or cardboard cutouts to be your characters, and shoot a Stop Motion film (there are a number of smartphone apps that can help you, if you go that way). Something around 1-2min will suffice, as long as it tells a coherent story. We expect it to have voices, sound effects, some sort of soundtrack and an opening and ending. Post a description of the techniques and tools you used to create the video, the script and, of course, the resulting video (or a link to it on youtube). Be original, tasteful, and, as always, we may ask for improvements/changes before accepting the result, especially if the quality or originality requirements aren't met.
