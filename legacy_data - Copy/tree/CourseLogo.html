Course Logo

A logo is an important part of the image of any entity or corporation. Good logos help people recognize a company or product and are an important form of branding. They should distill in a single and simple graphic the ideologies and nature of what they stand for. Good logos abound and are all around us:

For this challenge you'll need to create a logo for this course! And you'll need to do it as a vector graphic, in SVG (you can use Inkscape, for instance). There are many, many web pages that can guide you through the logo design process. Here's a few:

  • 70 Excellent Logo Design Tutorials and Resources
  • 10 Logo Design Tips for a Timeless and Creative Logo
  • LogoBee's Top 10 Logo Design Tips
  • 5 useful logo design tips
  • 10 Tips for Designing Logos That Don't Suck
  • Henri Ehrhart
  • How to Design a logo - Time Lapse Logo Design
You can also take a look at IST's new logo usage guidelines.

Especially, do keep in mind that:

  • A logo is an abstraction, not a photo or a detailed illustration (especially on this day and age where simple is better)
  • A corollary of the above: scale your logo to postal stamp size. Does it still work? If not, it is too complicated...
  • Simplicity goes for color, too
  • A logo should have something to do with what it represents
  • A logo should be memorable

What do we expect in your post? We want a rendered PNG version of your logo (add it in multiple backgrounds, etc. if it is versatile and you want to show it off), an attached SVG file of the logo, and at least two images of sketches and early versions/alternative ideas for the logo, to show you didn't just sit in front of Inscape and drew the first thing that came to mind. Optionally, you can craft a simple "logo usage guide" as the one linked above (for IST). In the PNGs, above, you must include at least a "postal stamp-sized" version, to prove it works.

As usual, you should present original work, and we the professors may ask for improvements/changes before accepting the result, especially if the quality or originality requirements aren't met.
